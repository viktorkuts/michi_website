# Michi — Product Context

register: brand

## What Michi is

Michi is a mobile app (iOS + Android) for finding, creating, and joining
real-world plans near you: pickup basketball, tennis sessions, camping
trips, club nights. No feed, no follower counts. You see what's happening
within reach, tap join, coordinate in a plan-scoped chat, and show up.

Operated from Quebec, Canada. Rolling out city by city, starting in the
Montreal area (gallery events: Parc Elm, Cap-Saint-Jacques, Mont-Tremblant,
La Ronde). Governed by Quebec Law 25 / PIPEDA — privacy is a real product
constraint, not marketing garnish.

## Users

18+ adults in launch cities who are tired of scrolling and short on
in-person plans. They don't need convincing that loneliness is a problem;
they need to believe this app gets them outside with near-zero effort.

## Product facts (verified against founder docs in
## OneDrive/Desktop/Synq-docs + app screenshots)

- Sign-up via Google or Apple account (NOT phone number).
- Create event: plus icon on Discover → place, time, one-line description.
- Joining: tap join; shared invites enter immediately, discovery joins
  need host approval.
- Chats are per-event and persist afterward: the chat is the club, and
  new events link to it (there are no recurring events as a feature).
- Friends capped at 365; contacts you don't interact with for ~1 year
  auto-decay off. Friends added only through shared events, group
  chats, or proximity sync; no people search. "Friend list" is a
  banned phrase per the brand voice doc.
- Event locations show the area only; exact address after joining.
- Profile: username, photo, bio, events joined/hosted, third places.
- Free, no ads at scale, no likes, no followers.
- Support email: contact@michi.quest. LinkedIn: company/111004970.
- App UI: cyan/turquoise accent with ink text on fills, editorial serif
  italic accents ("Basketball *Practice*"), soft off-white surfaces.

## Voice

Plainspoken, specific, a little dry. Short sentences. Concrete nouns
(parks, courts, Thursday) over abstractions ("community", "connection").
Never defensive, never flippant, never SaaS-speak. No em dashes.

## Anti-references

Meetup's corporate blandness; BeReal's ironic detachment; generic
AI-generated landing pages with icon-card grids and gradient text.
